Research

Current Research

Thesis cover coming soon

Urban Experimentation and Its Afterlife — The Stuttgart Superblock in Municipal Governance

M.Sc. Integrated Urbanism and Sustainable Design · University of Stuttgart

Submission: 18.09.2026

Supervisor: Prof. Dr.-Ing. Astrid Ley

Research Focus

The thesis investigates how a verkehrsberuhigendes Reallabor — the Stuttgart Superblock pilot in the Stadtmitte district — moves from temporary pilot into regelhafte kommunale Verwaltung. Using semi-structured interviews with administration, politics, and residents alongside document analysis of municipal decision processes, the work develops an analytical framework for the afterlife of urban experiments: which conditions allow a Reallabor to be institutionalised, and which cause it to evaporate when the temporary funding ends.

The findings speak directly to Smart City and Verwaltungsinnovation practice — how cities can build durable governance scaffolding around experimental urban formats and ensure that evidence-based pilots translate into sustained municipal policy.

Keywords:
Reallabore · Urban Experimentation · Municipal Governance · Verkehrsberuhigung · Verwaltungsinnovation · Stuttgart Superblock · Institutional Embedding · Governance Innovation

Vila Nova de Gaia

M.Sc. Studio · Strategic Urban Plan

Vila Nova de Gaia, Portugal — A Year-long M.Sc. Studio in Strategic Urban Planning

Year: 2025/26 · Location: Vila Nova de Gaia, Portugal · Type: Five-chapter M.Sc. studio project

Project Brief:

A year-long M.Sc. studio developed across five chapters: a six-dimensional methodology (drawn from sociology, political science, ecology); spatial diagnosis operationalising social-inclusion indicators; governance analysis across macro-regional, municipal, and neighbourhood scales; a strategic plan synthesising six dimensions and four interlocking challenges; and a community-managed hub embedded in existing public space.

Approach: The project combines rigorous stakeholder engagement, multi-scalar governance mapping, and equity-centred spatial analysis. The final deliverable articulates a strategic vision and actionable community hub that serves as an institutional anchor for participation and local stewardship.

Neckar Nexus

M.Sc. Studio · Strategic + Spatial

Neckar Nexus: The Neckar as a Mediator — Bridging Communities Through River-Centred Urban Planning

Year: 2025 · Location: Bad Cannstatt, Stuttgart · Type: Team of 4 (strategic + spatial)

Collaborators: Habiba Al Harrasi, Gréta Fazekas, Veronika Babiča

Supervisors: Dr. Shaharin Elham, Angie Carolina Camacho Gutiérrez, Prof. Dr.-Ing. Astrid Ley, Dr. Leonie Fischer

Project Brief: A strategic and spatial proposal for Bad Cannstatt's relationship with the Neckar, focused on the Arab community on the right bank. Who Gets the Green?

GIS Research · Spatial Inequality

Who Gets the Green? Spatial Inequality and the 3-30-300 Rule of Green-Space Access in London

Year: 2025 · Location: London, United Kingdom · Type: Team of 2 (GIS research)

Collaborator: Ayesha Parveen

Supervisor: Dr. Leonie Fischer

Project Brief: A GIS investigation of whether the 3-30-300 rule reveals spatial inequities in green-space access in London. Index of Multiple Deprivation (IMD) data joined to LSOA polygons; spatial analysis traces how wealth concentration and green-space access overlap. Findings argue for equity-weighted indicators in urban green-space planning.

Methodology: Open-data workflows combining deprivation indices, greenspace cadastres, and accessibility metrics. Results challenge universalist green-space planning assumptions and demonstrate how data-driven analysis can centre equity in climate-adaptation and livability planning.

Tags: GIS · Spatial Inequality · Green Infrastructure · Open Data · Urban Equity

Grijo em Comum

M.Sc. Studio · Community Cohesion

Grijo em Comum: From Coexistence to Cohesion

Location: Grijo, Vila Nova de Gaia, Portugal · Year: 2025/26 · Type: M.Sc. Studio

Supervisors: Prof. Dr. Astrid Ley, Els Keunen

Collaborators: Felipe González-Zapata, Jasper Schmidt, Nadeen Hesham, Yara Mohammed

Project Brief: The phrase "From Coexistence to Cohesion" is simple. What it names is not. In peri-urban typology like Grijo, the problem is not that people distrust each other—it is that they barely meet. The elderly, members of the Roma community, recent migrants, and local workers share the same streets, the same shops, the same municipal services. But they inhabit entirely separate social worlds, intersecting only in transactional spaces, completely missing meaningful contact that builds trust or collective agency.

Research Insight: From our observations and research, we call this condition "Coexistence without Inclusion" — and we have a response to it. The project develops spatial and social interventions that create genuine meeting points, foster intercultural exchange, and build institutional frameworks for sustained community cohesion.

Tags: Strategic Planning · Community Cohesion · Migration · Peri-urban · Intercultural Exchange · Social Inclusion
